WHO ARE WE?

France has a rich history of café culture that dates back to the 17th century. The iconic Café Procope, opened in Paris in 1994, was the first shop to sell coffee that wasn’t from a cart on the street. However, it wasn’t the drink itself that enticed its patrons, but the community it fostered. The lush and inviting interior encouraged people to gather and linger, and the Café Procope became a base for resistance and revolution. It was a clubhouse of sorts for the artistic and political rabble-rousers of the time.
